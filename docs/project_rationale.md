# Project rationale

## Purpose

This document sets out the scientific rationale for a staged post-PhD research programme focused on cross-modal biological triangulation of disease-prioritised regional genetic components in neurodegenerative disease.

The aim is not to claim that regional genetic components prove causal mechanisms. Instead, the aim is to define a conservative framework for testing whether selected components can be followed as reproducible liability-indexing axes across imaging, molecular and clinical domains.

## Background

Late-onset neurodegenerative diseases present a difficult post-GWAS interpretation problem. Common genetic susceptibility is distributed, linkage disequilibrium creates regional dependence, and clinical diagnoses are indirect, heterogeneous endpoints.

Single-variant association testing remains essential for locus discovery, but additional intermediate representations may be useful for organising regional genetic evidence and linking it to downstream biological phenotypes.

## Origin of this programme

This programme grows out of PhD work developing and applying LD-constrained local independent component analysis (LD-CLICA), an LD-aware framework for modelling structured local genotype variation in neurodegenerative disease.

The next stage is to test whether selected disease-prioritised components can be characterised through independent replication, molecular annotation, imaging follow-up and conditional refinement.

## Central question

Can pre-specified regional genetic components be tested for convergent evidence across independent imaging cohorts, transcriptomic and epigenomic resources, proteomic data, fine-mapping analyses and clinical phenotypes?

## Working hypothesis

Disease-prioritised regional genetic components may index reproducible biological liability axes that can be characterised across imaging, molecular and clinical domains.

This hypothesis is deliberately bounded. It does not assume that such components are causal, mechanistically specific, or clinically actionable without further validation.

## Initial programme stages

1. Component-to-molecular annotation.
2. Independent imaging replication and extension.
3. Molecular-imaging convergence analysis.
4. Conditional and causal refinement only after replication.

## Scope boundaries

This programme distinguishes between:

- exploratory analysis;
- confirmatory replication;
- mechanistic interpretation;
- causal inference.

Only replicated and convergent findings should be advanced toward mechanistic or causal interpretation.
